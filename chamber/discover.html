    • Introduction

    Mutare is picturesquely situated in the foothills of the Eastern Highlands sprawling up the sides of Christmas Pass. It has been known for its wide streets lined with flowering trees, three public parks, a museum (1954), and the Turner Memorial Library (1902)

    Industry

    Industry includes automobile assembly, the manufacture of textiles, clothing, leather goods, pulp, and board, and oil refining and wattle (acacia) extraction.

    Tourism

    Tourism to the nearby national parks is an important economic facto

    Transport

    a port of entry and the main rail and commercial centre for the productive eastern region (tea, tobacco, livestock, timber)

    Mining

    In recent years the mining industry now defines the city and it's economy. Recent discovery of large deposits of surface diamonds have redefined the city that it has earned the coloqial name "Ngoda City". Which means city of diamonds. It is the only city and region in the country with diamonds. The majority of diamonds in the city are black diamonds which are mined in shallow open mines and in some areas they can literally be picked from the ground. In addition several gold deposits are found thoughout the city
